# TRIM-HAA Participant Core Guide

Expected session length: approximately 60-90 minutes, including instructions, practice, breaks, annotation tasks, and feedback questions. This duration range is provisional and must be finalised before ethics submission and recruitment.

This guide explains the fields you will complete. It uses synthetic examples only. You do not need to produce private internal reasoning. The short notes you write are submitted explanations for the research record.

Use the participant label guide for the available function labels. Use the participant mechanism guide for the available rationale mechanisms.

## Primary Evidence

Question: Which smallest part or parts of the text most directly support your current judgment?

Select only the segment or segments that are most directly needed.

Synthetic example: if the text says, "Mira locked the door, then checked the empty hallway twice," and your judgment is about caution, you might select the segment about checking the hallway twice.

## Function Label

Question: Which available category best describes the role or function you identify in this case?

Choose one category from the participant label guide unless the final study guide says otherwise. If no category fits and a no-fit option is available, use that option.

## Rationale Mechanism

Question: How does your selected evidence support that label?

The rationale mechanism is the type of connection between the text you selected and the label you chose. For example, evidence might support a label by confirming an earlier idea, showing contrast, repeating a pattern, or escalating a situation. Use the participant mechanism guide for the exact options.

## Uncertainty

Question: How uncertain are you about the annotation you are submitting?

Use one of:

- Low
- Medium
- High

Uncertainty is not failure. Use higher uncertainty when the evidence is ambiguous, the category boundary is unclear, or another defensible reading remains.

## Rationale Note

Question: Briefly explain the connection between your evidence, mechanism, and label.

Synthetic example: "The repeated hallway check supports caution because it shows the character actively checking safety rather than simply noticing the hallway."

## Alternative Pathway

Question: Is there another complete and defensible interpretation that you still think remains possible?

An alternative is not mandatory. It is not simply a vague doubt. It should be another possible path that has evidence, a label, and a mechanism. Uncertainty and an alternative are related but not identical: you may be uncertain without having a full alternative, and you may retain both uncertainty and an alternative.

If you mark an alternative as present:

- Select the alternative mechanism.
- Write a short alternative note.

Synthetic example: "Alternative: the repeated hallway check could show suspicion rather than caution, because the action may imply distrust of someone nearby."

## What Not To Include

Do not write personal biographical details or private internal reasoning. Keep notes focused on the submitted annotation.

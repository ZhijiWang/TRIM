# TRIM-HAA Contamination and Eligibility Screen

Use this screen before formal pilot participation. Do not automatically reuse historical coder submissions.

Expected session length: approximately 60-90 minutes, including instructions, practice, breaks, annotation tasks, and feedback questions. This duration range is provisional and must be finalised before ethics submission and recruitment.

## Screening Questions

Have you previously seen any of the following?

- The AI outputs for this pilot
- Earlier coder records for these cases
- The study manuscript
- Adjudicated cases
- Detailed case analyses
- Previous versions of this pilot

Also ask:

- Have you previously read any TRIM-HAA project document?
- Have you previously seen any cases used in this study?
- Have you previously seen any AI annotations used in this study?
- Were you involved in designing or testing this study?
- Before today, what did you think this study was mainly investigating?

## Practice-Case Eligibility

After the practice case, record one outcome:

- `ready_for_formal_pilot`
- `ready_with_documented_support`
- `usability_feedback_only`
- `stop_session`

If the participant cannot distinguish function label from rationale mechanism after standardised re-explanation, exclude from formal pilot data collection and retain only usability feedback if consent permits.

## Classification

Eligible:

- No prior exposure to pilot materials or AI outputs, and practice outcome is `ready_for_formal_pilot`.

Eligible with disclosure:

- General familiarity with the topic but no access to AI outputs, records, adjudications, or exact cases, and practice outcome is `ready_with_documented_support`.

Exclude from formal pilot:

- Prior exposure to AI outputs, adjudicated cases, earlier coder records for the same cases, or inability to meet the practice-case standard.

Retain only as usability tester:

- Prior involvement in developing the instrument, manuscript, or pilot materials, or practice outcome is `usability_feedback_only`.

Record the classification and reason using the pseudonymous participant ID only.

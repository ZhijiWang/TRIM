# Claim Boundaries
## Supported by this comparison

- The final labels differ.
- Evidence selection partially overlaps.
- Uncertainty differs.
- Both records retain two interpretive or causal pathways.
- Primary and alternative emphasis are inverted under the operational definition for this locked-record comparison.
- A final-label-only comparison would omit shared interpretive structure.
- The current schema can represent this structured difference.

## Not supported by this comparison

- One interpretation is correct.
- One annotator is superior.
- The model misunderstood the text.
- The author identified the true killer.
- The medium deliberately deceived anyone.
- Third-party homicide is established.
- Self-inflicted death is established as the complete causal account.
- AI assistance changed human judgment.
- TRIM-HAA is empirically validated.
- This single case generalises to other texts, languages, models, or annotators.
- Evidence overlap proves semantic agreement.
- Label disagreement proves completely different interpretations.

## Requires additional cases or human evaluation

- Independent bilingual human review.
- Multiple cases.
- Multiple human coders.
- Multiple models or preregistered model runs.
- Preregistered comparison rules.
- Adjudication.
- Reliability analysis.
- Exposure-condition study.
- Broader multilingual validation.

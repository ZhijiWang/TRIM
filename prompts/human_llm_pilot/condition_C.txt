Controlled friction_locus vocabulary, using the frozen lineage table as category-definition source:
- cue_function: use only when the cue family itself remains the dominant unresolved converter after more specific loci are excluded.
- warrant_attribution: use when one source, medium, speaker, sign, or result is granted warranting force for the function label.
- warrant_relation: use when conflict, ranking, qualification, suspension, or interaction among multiple warrants is the dominant procedural locus.
- operation_function: use when a visible interpretive, testimonial, consultative, recognitional, or self-reading operation converts evidence to function.
- boundary_setting: use when the relevant case, interpretive unit, category boundary, speaker boundary, or scope of application is unstable.
- temporal_layering: use when later fulfilment, retrospective framing, historical readability, or reuse across time changes the function of earlier evidence.
- perspective_assignment: use when speaker, witness role, testimonial position, focalization, or narrative standpoint determines the function.
- context_inference: use when a contextual bridge beyond the local anchor is the dominant obstacle to evidence-to-function conversion.
- unresolved: use when the record cannot responsibly choose a controlled category under the frozen instructions.


Frozen counterfactual-test and confusable-with guidance:
- cue_function -> operation_function: A cue type such as prophecy, testimony, or divination may be mistaken for the visible act performed with it. Counterfactual test: Ask whether the problem is what this cue type does here, or what an act of interpretation, consultation, confession, recognition, or self-reading does. Expected risk: Reserved cue_function may be overused when operation_function is the more specific locus. Prior examples: operation_function has prior positive examples; cue_function has no positive demo example. Directionality: directional
- cue_function -> warrant_attribution: A cue family may be confused with the source or medium receiving warranting force. Counterfactual test: Ask whether the cue type itself is underdetermined after excluding more specific loci, or whether one source, sign, medium, speaker, or result is granted standing. Expected risk: Reserved cue_function may be overused when warrant_attribution is the more specific locus. Prior examples: warrant_attribution has prior positive examples; cue_function has no positive demo example. Directionality: directional
- warrant_attribution -> warrant_relation: A single source granted authority may be confused with an interaction among multiple warrants. Counterfactual test: Count the warrants: if the annotation depends on how more than one warrant interacts, use warrant_relation; if one source receives authority, use warrant_attribution. Expected risk: One-warrant cases may be overcoded as warrant_relation when contextual complexity is present. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- warrant_attribution -> operation_function: Authority is often assigned through an interpretive act, so granting standing may be confused with visible operation. Counterfactual test: If evidence becomes usable as a warrant through the operation, prefer warrant_attribution; if evidence already has standing and the function depends on handling it, prefer operation_function. Expected risk: Warrant attribution may be overcoded as operation_function when interpretation is visibly present. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- warrant_relation -> temporal_layering: Cross-warrant conflict may coexist with retrospective framing or later readability. Counterfactual test: If removing cross-warrant conflict removes the function, prefer warrant_relation; if removing the later temporal frame removes it, prefer temporal_layering. Expected risk: Warrant conflicts in retrospectively framed cases may be mistaken for temporal_layering, or temporal effects may be flattened into warrant_relation. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- warrant_relation -> perspective_assignment: Incompatible testimony or standpoint can look like conflict among warrants or like perspective control. Counterfactual test: Ask whether incompatibility among multiple warrants determines the function, or whether changing the speaker/testimonial position would change the annotation. Expected risk: Perspective-governed testimony may be overcoded as warrant_relation when multiple accounts are visible. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- operation_function -> warrant_relation: An operation can matter because it performs conversion or because it creates, exposes, ranks, or intensifies relations among warrants. Counterfactual test: If the operation itself performs the conversion, prefer operation_function; if it mainly relates multiple warrants, prefer warrant_relation. Expected risk: Operations that rank warrants may be overcoded as operation_function when warrant_relation is dominant. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- operation_function -> temporal_layering: Recognition or fulfilment can be treated as a local operation or as later reclassification of earlier evidence. Counterfactual test: If the local operation produces the function, prefer operation_function; if a later moment reclassifies earlier evidence, prefer temporal_layering. Expected risk: Later reclassification may be overcoded as operation_function when a recognition scene is visible. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- boundary_setting -> warrant_relation: A scope or unit boundary may be confused with the relation among warrants inside a stable unit. Counterfactual test: Ask whether changing the boundary of the annotation would change the result; if the boundary is stable but warrants interact, use warrant_relation. Expected risk: Boundary_setting may be underused when coders move directly to warrant_relation inside an unstable unit. Prior examples: warrant_relation has prior positive examples; boundary_setting has no positive demo example. Directionality: directional
- boundary_setting -> context_inference: A missing boundary may be confused with a missing contextual bridge. Counterfactual test: Ask whether the relevant unit/scope is unstable, or whether the unit is stable but needs external contextual inference to support conversion. Expected risk: Boundary problems may be absorbed into context_inference as a residual explanation. Prior examples: neither boundary_setting nor context_inference has a positive demo example in the cited manual lineage. Directionality: directional
- temporal_layering -> warrant_relation: Later fulfilment, retrospective readability, or reuse may coexist with local warrant conflict. Counterfactual test: If removing the later temporal frame removes the function, prefer temporal_layering; if removing cross-warrant conflict removes it, prefer warrant_relation. Expected risk: Temporal_layering may be undercoded when later readability appears alongside multiple warrants. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- temporal_layering -> operation_function: A visible recognition or fulfilment scene can look like a local operation. Counterfactual test: If the function is produced by local operation, prefer operation_function; if a later moment reclassifies earlier evidence, prefer temporal_layering. Expected risk: Temporal effects may be overcoded as operation_function when the text includes a visible recognition act. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- perspective_assignment -> warrant_relation: Perspective, witness role, or testimonial position may be confused with incompatibility among multiple accounts as warrants. Counterfactual test: Ask whether changing the speaker/testimonial position would change the annotation; if incompatibility among multiple warrants is dominant, use warrant_relation. Expected risk: Perspective_assignment may be undercoded when multiple accounts make warrant conflict salient. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- perspective_assignment -> operation_function: Confession or testimony can be treated as a visible operation or as standpoint-governed perspective. Counterfactual test: Ask whether the function depends on the act of confessing/testifying or on whose perspective/testimonial role controls the annotation. Expected risk: Perspective-governed testimony may be overcoded as operation_function when the speech act is prominent. Prior examples: both have prior positive examples. Directionality: symmetric
- context_inference -> temporal_layering: Historical readability or later framing may be mistaken for external contextual inference. Counterfactual test: Ask whether later readability is the decisive feature; if yes, use temporal_layering rather than context_inference. Expected risk: Context_inference may be overused when temporal_layering provides the more specific explanation. Prior examples: temporal_layering has prior positive examples; context_inference has no positive demo example. Directionality: directional
- context_inference -> warrant_relation: External or background context may be confused with interaction among warrants within the text. Counterfactual test: Ask whether the local text contains interacting warrants; if yes, use warrant_relation. Use context_inference only when the missing contextual bridge is dominant. Expected risk: Context_inference may be overused as a residual category for difficult warrant interactions. Prior examples: warrant_relation has prior positive examples; context_inference has no positive demo example. Directionality: directional

For every relevant confusable pair, record a counterfactual_tests entry with test_id, question, answer, cited_evidence, effect_on_decision, and confidence. Ask whether changing or removing the proposed source of distinction would change the assignment. Do not invent examples from the study sample.

Reserved-category and escalation rules:
Reserved or review-required categories may be proposed, but they must use friction_locus_operational_status = requires_human_review and final_operational_label = unresolved. Escalation is required when the category is reserved, a proposed category is outside the controlled vocabulary, evidence IDs are not present in the packet, counterfactual tests do not resolve the assignment, or parse repair would require substantive rewriting.

Disagreement-decomposition reminder:
Records may later differ in evidence selection, counterfactual answers, decision route, friction_locus, primary label, uncertainty, alternatives, or escalation status. Your task is to preserve the procedure, not to optimize for agreement.

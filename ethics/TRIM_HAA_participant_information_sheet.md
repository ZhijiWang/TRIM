# TRIM-HAA Participant Information Sheet

## Study Title

TRIM-HAA: Human-AI Annotation Audit Instrumentation Pilot

## Research Purpose

This study tests whether a small annotation workflow is understandable and usable. It examines whether locked independent records and second-pass records can be collected, linked, and compared across labels, evidence, mechanisms, uncertainty, alternatives, and revision notes.

## Why You Are Invited

You are invited because you are an adult who may be able to complete short interpretive annotation tasks in the selected language.

## What Participation Involves

You will read short segmented cases, complete structured annotation records, and complete short feedback questions. In some cases, you may see a frozen AI-generated annotation before submitting a second-pass record. In other cases, you will not see AI output.

The AI-generated annotations are research stimuli and are not treated as correct answers.

The session structure includes information and consent, background questions, instructions, a practice case, annotation cases, revision-reason questions, burden and comprehension questions, and debriefing. Exact duration will be estimated conservatively before scheduling and refined after approved pilot use.

## Voluntary Participation

Participation is voluntary. You may stop at any time. You may skip questions where the form permits skipping.

## Possible Inconvenience

The task may be tiring, repetitive, or mildly frustrating. AI output may be confusing or unhelpful.

## Benefits

There is no guaranteed direct personal benefit.

## Confidentiality

Research records will use pseudonymous participant IDs. Legal names will not be stored in the research dataset.

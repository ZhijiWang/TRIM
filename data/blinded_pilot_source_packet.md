# Blinded Pilot Source Packet

## Coder Instructions

Read each case as an independent passage. Use the linked full scene or testimony
section whenever the excerpt begins or ends mid-scene. Record your own evidence
selection in `data/blinded_pilot_coding_template.csv`.

The packet gives source text, location, and neutral comprehension support. It
contains no primary-coder TRIM fields, comparison results, or worked
interpretations.

For the Classical Chinese cases, the English notes are fresh, close paraphrases
prepared for this pilot rather than copied from a published translation. Coders
able to read Classical Chinese should treat the original text as primary.

## `ZZ_XIANG_7`

**Source:** *Zuo zhuan*, Lord Xiang, year 7  
**Language:** Classical Chinese  
**Case unit:** the suburban-sacrifice divination and Meng Xianzi's response

### Original text

> 季夏，三卜郊，不從，乃免牲。孟獻子曰：「吾乃今而後知有卜筮。夫郊祀后稷，以祈農事也。是故啟蟄而郊，郊而後耕。今既耕而卜郊，宜其不從也。」

### Close paraphrase

In the last month of summer, Lu divined three times about conducting the
suburban sacrifice, and the results did not comply, so the sacrificial animal
was released. Meng Xianzi says that he now understands the point of divination.
The suburban sacrifice honours Houji and seeks success in farming; it should be
performed after the awakening of insects and before ploughing. Since ploughing
has already taken place, it is fitting that the divination did not comply.

### Location note

Read the consultation, the release of the animal, and Meng Xianzi's full ritual
timing explanation as one case.

## `ZZ_MIN_1`

**Source:** *Zuo zhuan*, Lord Min, year 1  
**Language:** Classical Chinese  
**Case unit:** Bi Wan's milfoil consultation about entering service in Jin

### Original text

> 初，畢萬筮仕於晉，遇屯之比。辛廖占之，曰：「吉。屯固比入，吉孰大焉？其必蕃昌。震為土，車從馬，足居之，兄長之，母覆之，眾歸之，六體不易。合而能固，安而能殺，公侯之卦也。公侯之子孫，必復其始。」

### Close paraphrase

Earlier, Bi Wan used milfoil to ask about taking office in Jin and encountered
the change from *Zhun* to *Bi*. Xin Liao judges the result auspicious. He reads
the two hexagrams through stability and entry, then develops the figures of
earth, carriage, horse, feet, elder brother, mother, and the gathering of the
many. He concludes that the configuration is associated with lords and predicts
that the descendants of a lord will return to their beginning.

### Location note

The case includes the reported hexagram change, Xin Liao's full explanation,
and the concluding statement about descendants.

## `ZZ_XI_4`

**Source:** *Zuo zhuan*, Lord Xi, year 4  
**Language:** Classical Chinese  
**Case unit:** Lord Xian's consultations about establishing Li Ji as wife

### Original text

> 初，晉獻公欲以驪姬為夫人，卜之，不吉；筮之，吉。公曰：「從筮。」卜人曰：「筮短龜長，不如從長。且其繇曰：『專之渝，攘公之羭。一薰一蕕，十年尚猶有臭。』必不可！」弗聽，立之。

### Close paraphrase

Earlier, Lord Xian of Jin wished to establish Li Ji as his wife. Turtle-shell
divination was inauspicious, while milfoil divination was auspicious. He says
that he will follow the milfoil result. The turtle-shell diviner replies that
milfoil is short and the turtle long, so the longer should be followed. He also
quotes the divinatory wording, which describes an exclusive attachment changing,
a lord's ram being taken, and the lingering smell produced when a fragrant plant
and a foul weed are placed together. He insists that the marriage must not be
made. Lord Xian does not listen and establishes Li Ji.

### Location note

Keep the two reported results, Lord Xian's choice, the diviner's response, the
quoted wording, and the final decision together.

## `ZZ_ZHUANG_22`

**Source:** *Zuo zhuan*, Lord Zhuang, year 22  
**Language:** Classical Chinese  
**Case unit:** the two divinatory scenes attached to Jingzhong

### Original text A: marriage divination

> 初，懿氏卜妻敬仲，其妻占之，曰：「吉。是謂鳳皇于飛，和鳴鏘鏘。有媯之後，將育于姜。五世其昌，並于正卿；八世之後，莫之與京。」

### Original text B: Zhouyi consultation

> 陳厲公，蔡出也。故蔡人殺五父而立之。生敬仲。其少也，周史有以《周易》見陳侯者，陳侯使筮之，遇《觀》之《否》。曰：「是謂觀國之光，利用賓于王。此其代陳有國乎？不在此，其在異國；非此其身，在其子孫。光，遠而自他有耀者也。坤，土也；巽，風也；乾，天也。風為天於土上，山也。有山之材，而照之以天光，於是乎居土上，故曰觀國之光，利用賓于王。庭實旅百，奉之以玉帛，天地之美具焉，故曰利用賓于王。猶有觀焉，故曰其在後乎。風行而著於土，故曰其在異國乎。若在異國，必姜姓也。姜，大嶽之後也。山嶽則配天。物莫能兩大，陳衰，此其昌乎！」

### Close paraphrase

The first scene reports a marriage divination for Jingzhong. It is judged
auspicious and described through paired phoenixes, a descendant of Gui being
nurtured among Jiang, flourishing after five generations, reaching the level of
the chief ministers, and becoming unmatched after eight generations.

The second scene recalls a Zhou historian presenting the *Zhouyi* to the lord of
Chen when Jingzhong was young. The consultation produces a change from *Guan* to
*Pi*. The interpretation begins from the phrase about viewing the splendour of a
state and being received as a guest by a king. It asks whether the result concerns
rule in Chen, redirects the future to another state and to descendants, develops
images of light, earth, wind, heaven, mountain, gifts, and court reception, and
concludes by asking whether Jingzhong's line will flourish when Chen declines,
probably in a Jiang-surnamed state.

### Location note

Treat both scenes as part of the case. Preserve their sequence and the movement
between the immediate actors and later generations.

## `MAC_1_3`

**Source:** William Shakespeare, *Macbeth*, Act 1, Scene 3  
**Language:** English  
**Full scene:** https://shakespeare.mit.edu/macbeth/macbeth.1.3.html

### Passage

> SECOND WITCH: All hail, Macbeth, hail to thee, thane of Cawdor!
>
> THIRD WITCH: All hail, Macbeth, thou shalt be king hereafter!
>
> MACBETH: Stay, you imperfect speakers, tell me more: / By Sinel's death I
> know I am thane of Glamis; / But how of Cawdor? the thane of Cawdor lives,
> / A prosperous gentleman; and to be king / Stands not within the prospect
> of belief, / No more than to be Cawdor.
>
> [Enter ROSS and ANGUS]
>
> ROSS: And, for an earnest of a greater honour, / He bade me, from him, call
> thee thane of Cawdor: / In which addition, hail, most worthy thane! / For it
> is thine.
>
> MACBETH [Aside]: Glamis, and thane of Cawdor! / The greatest is behind.
>
> BANQUO: That trusted home / Might yet enkindle you unto the crown, / Besides
> the thane of Cawdor. But 'tis strange: / And oftentimes, to win us to our
> harm, / The instruments of darkness tell us truths, / Win us with honest
> trifles, to betray's / In deepest consequence.
>
> MACBETH [Aside]: Two truths are told, / As happy prologues to the swelling
> act / Of the imperial theme.

### Location note

Read the witches' greeting, Macbeth's initial response, the arrival of Ross and
Angus, the announcement of Cawdor, and the subsequent asides together.

## `MAC_4_1`

**Source:** William Shakespeare, *Macbeth*, Act 4, Scene 1  
**Language:** English  
**Full scene:** https://shakespeare.mit.edu/macbeth/macbeth.4.1.html

### Passage

> FIRST APPARITION: Macbeth! Macbeth! Macbeth! beware Macduff; / Beware the
> thane of Fife. Dismiss me. Enough.
>
> SECOND APPARITION: Be bloody, bold, and resolute; laugh to scorn / The power
> of man, for none of woman born / Shall harm Macbeth.
>
> MACBETH: Then live, Macduff: what need I fear of thee? / But yet I'll make
> assurance double sure, / And take a bond of fate: thou shalt not live.
>
> THIRD APPARITION: Be lion-mettled, proud; and take no care / Who chafes, who
> frets, or where conspirers are: / Macbeth shall never vanquish'd be until /
> Great Birnam wood to high Dunsinane hill / Shall come against him.
>
> MACBETH: That will never be. / Who can impress the forest, bid the tree /
> Unfix his earth-bound root? Sweet bodements! good! / Rebellion's head, rise
> never till the wood / Of Birnam rise, and our high-placed Macbeth / Shall
> live the lease of nature.

### Location note

Read the warnings and conditions together with Macbeth's immediate responses.

## `MAC_5_8`

**Language:** English  
**Full scene:** https://shakespeare.mit.edu/macbeth/macbeth.5.8.html

### Passage

> MACBETH: Thou losest labour: / As easy mayst thou the intrenchant air /
> With thy keen sword impress as make me bleed: / Let fall thy blade on
> vulnerable crests; / I bear a charmed life, which must not yield, / To one
> of woman born.
>
> MACDUFF: Despair thy charm; / And let the angel whom thou still hast served
> / Tell thee, Macduff was from his mother's womb / Untimely ripp'd.
>
> MACBETH: Accursed be that tongue that tells me so, / For it hath cow'd my
> better part of man! / And be these juggling fiends no more believed, / That

> ear, / And break it to our hope.

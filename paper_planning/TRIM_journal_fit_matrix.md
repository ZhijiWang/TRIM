## Scenario-Based Rankings

Do not use one rigid journal ranking independent of empirical outcome. The
target should depend on what the completed v0.2.1 retest shows.

### Scenario A: Retest Yields Theoretically Interesting Pathway Results

Use this route if the retest produces strong examples of same-function/different
pathway, different-function/shared pathway, or disagreement categories that
substantively illuminate computational literary interpretation.

1. Journal of Computational Literary Studies
2. Digital Humanities Quarterly
3. Digital Scholarship in the Humanities
4. Journal of Cultural Analytics
5. Digital Studies / Le champ numérique

### Scenario B: Retest Mainly Demonstrates Method Refinement And Infrastructure

Use this route if the retest chiefly shows that the corrected package is usable,
source-text grounded, leakage-controlled, and analytically inspectable, but the
pathway findings are more methodological than theoretical.

1. Digital Humanities Quarterly
2. Digital Studies / Le champ numérique
3. Journal of Computational Literary Studies
4. International Journal of Humanities and Arts Computing
5. Digital Scholarship in the Humanities

## Serious Candidate Details

### Journal of Computational Literary Studies

- **Publisher/platform:** JCLS; site protected by Anubis during review, but article metadata visible in search results.
- **Scope:** computational literary studies, operationalization, literary modelling, computational interpretation.
- **Comparable recent articles visible in search metadata:** "Operationalization and Interpretation Dependence in Computational..." (2025); "From Readers to Data. Uncertainty in Computational Literary Citizen Science" (2025); "Event Detection between Literary Studies and NLP" (2025); "Interpretation, Argument, Evaluation. A Workflow for Assessing LLM..." (2026/2025 metadata); "What Do Characters Do? The Embodied Agency of Fictional Characters" (Piper, 2023).
- **Best TRIM version:** CLS methods article on evidence-to-function pathways and structured interpretive disagreement.
- **Retest needed:** yes.
- **Fit score:** 8.5.

### Digital Humanities Quarterly

- **Publisher:** Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations and Association for Computers and the Humanities.
- **Official scope/guidelines:** DHQ accepts articles, reviews, case studies, and field reports; submissions must contribute to DH research/practice, communicate to a broad DH readership, and have an argument. It supports supplementary datasets and XML/TEI publication.
- **Word range:** no fixed upper limit, but official guidance warns that reader attention is finite.
- **OA:** DHQ content is openly available; site notes CC BY-ND 4.0 unless otherwise noted.
- **Comparable articles:** Schumacher and Fluh 2023 on manual annotation and gender category systems in literary texts; Dobson 2021 on interpretable outputs; Kleymann and Stange 2021 on hermeneutic visualization; Budzise-Weaver 2016 on qualitative coding of visual artwork; 2024/2025 issues show ongoing DH methods work.
- **Best TRIM version:** broad DH methods article with strong examples and candid pilot limits.
- **Retest needed:** recommended; a proof-of-concept could be considered if framed carefully.
- **Fit score:** 8.

### Digital Scholarship in the Humanities

- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press for EADH.
- **Official scope/guidelines:** international peer-reviewed journal publishing theoretical, methodological, experimental, and applied digital scholarship. Official page says DSH does not publish work-in-progress reports or descriptions of projects/tools/techniques/methodologies.
- **Word range:** full papers normally max 9,000 words excluding notes/references; short papers max 5,000.
- **OA/APC:** hybrid OA option; OA charge required if selected.
- **Data/code:** data availability statement required; data/software strongly encouraged where feasible.
- **Comparable current signals:** latest items include DH definitions, AI-generated text detection, thematic modelling, and historical spatiotemporal analysis. This indicates method/theory is possible, but empirical/theoretical payoff must be clear.
- **Best TRIM version:** completed retest article arguing that final-label agreement is methodologically insufficient, with TRIM as tested pathway representation.
- **Retest needed:** yes.
- **Fit score:** 7.5.

### Journal of Open Research Software

- **Publisher:** Ubiquity Press.
- **Official article types:** Software Metapapers and Issues in Research Software.
- **Word range:** Issues in Research Software 3,000-4,000 words; software metapapers use template.
- **OA/APC:** official page lists GBP 800 for Software Metapapers and GBP 865 for Issues in Research Software.
- **Code/data:** software must be freely available in a public repository under an open license.
- **Best TRIM version:** software metapaper after release/Zenodo DOI.
- **Retest needed:** no, but examples and uptake strengthen the case.
- **Fit score:** 5.

### Journal of Open Humanities Data

- **Publisher:** Ubiquity Press.
- **Official/search-verified scope:** peer-reviewed publications describing humanities research objects or techniques with high reuse potential.
- **Word range:** search-result metadata for official submission page lists Data Papers 1,000-1,500 words and Discussion Papers 3,000-5,000 words.
- **OA/APC:** Ubiquity APC table lists GBP 760 for JOHD as of July 3, 2024.
- **Best TRIM version:** data/retest package article, not the main theory/method paper.
- **Retest needed:** helpful to make the dataset complete.
- **Fit score:** 5.5.

## Best Choices

1. **Best intellectual fit:** Journal of Computational Literary Studies.
2. **Best realistic target:** Digital Humanities Quarterly.
3. **Best open-access target:** Digital Humanities Quarterly; JOHD if the release is data-package oriented.
4. **Best software/infrastructure fallback:** Journal of Open Research Software.
5. **Avoid for now:** Qualitative Research, Field Methods, Sociological Methods & Research, top literary theory journals, general comparative literature journals.

## Submission Strategy

Preferred route:

1. Complete v0.2.1 retest.
2. Draft for JCLS if the retest produces interesting pathway-level findings.
3. If JCLS framing feels too computational-literary, adapt to DHQ.
4. Keep DSH as a stretch only if the theoretical argument is sharpened and the article does not read like a project/tool report.
5. Prepare a separate JORS or JOHD fallback only after release if the methods article stalls.

# TRIM-HAA Ethics Submission Summary

Status: draft for institutional ethics review or exemption assessment. This document does not claim exemption, low-risk approval, or ethics approval.

## Study Rationale

The pilot tests whether TRIM-HAA can collect and compare independent human-pre, AI-generated, human-post, and no-AI second-pass records without excessive burden or ambiguous provenance.

## Design

The study is a small instrumentation and feasibility pilot with 4 to 6 adult participants and 4 to 6 short cases. Participants complete Core annotation records, then complete second-pass records either after seeing frozen AI output or after rereading without AI output.

## Participants

Participants are adults able to read the selected source language and complete structured annotation tasks. Specialist expertise is not required unless selected cases require it.

## AI Involvement

AI records are frozen before sessions. No live model generation occurs during participant sessions. AI-generated annotations are research stimuli and are not correct answers.

## Data Collected

The pilot collects pseudonymous structured annotations, provenance records, exposure-event records, lock records, background responses, revision-reason responses, burden and comprehension responses, timing fields if approved, and protocol-deviation logs.

## Risks and Safeguards

Main risks are fatigue, frustration, perceived evaluation, pressure to agree with AI, privacy of free text, and data security. Safeguards include voluntary participation, pseudonymous IDs, non-leading researcher script, de-identification review, encrypted storage, and clear withdrawal procedures.

## Consent and Withdrawal

Mandatory consent covers participation and required research records. Optional consent covers de-identified quotation and public release of structured annotations. Withdrawal limits must follow institutional policy.

## Dissemination

Findings may be reported in aggregate. De-identified excerpts and structured annotations are shared only under the relevant consent and review conditions.

## Institutional Determination

Whether this study is exempt, low risk, or requires full review remains an institutional determination.

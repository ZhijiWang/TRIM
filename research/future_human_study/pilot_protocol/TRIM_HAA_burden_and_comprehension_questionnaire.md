# TRIM-HAA Burden and Comprehension Questionnaire

Expected session length: approximately 60-90 minutes, including instructions, practice, breaks, annotation tasks, and feedback questions. This duration range is provisional and must be finalised before ethics submission and recruitment.

Use short response scales and optional free text. Self-report is not treated as objective proof of behaviour.

## Main Response Scale

Strongly disagree, Disagree, Neither agree nor disagree, Agree, Strongly agree.

## Clarity Items

1. The Core instructions were clear.
2. I could distinguish the function label from the rationale mechanism, meaning the connection between evidence and label.
3. I understood what counted as an alternative pathway.
4. I understood that leaving my earlier answer unchanged was acceptable.
5. I understood that the AI-generated response was not an answer key.

## Burden Items

6. Selecting primary evidence was difficult.
7. Selecting a function label was difficult.
8. Selecting a rationale mechanism was difficult.
9. Using the uncertainty field was difficult.
10. Deciding whether an alternative pathway existed was difficult.
11. Writing the rationale note required substantial effort.
12. The number of fields felt manageable.
13. I felt fatigued during the task.
14. The workflow felt repetitive.
15. I would be willing to complete a longer version of this task.

## Perceived Influence Items

16. The AI-generated response felt difficult to ignore.
17. The AI-generated response felt helpful.
18. Seeing both my earlier response and the AI-generated response was confusing.
19. I felt ownership of my final response.

## Wording-Overlap Item

Response scale: Not at all, A little, Some, A lot, Not applicable.

20. Some wording in my final rationale was taken from or adapted from the AI-generated response.

## Comprehension Check

The AI-generated annotation was presented as:

- The correct answer
- One possible annotation
- The researcher's preferred answer
- Not sure

Correct response for analysis: one possible annotation.

## Open Questions

1. Which Core fields, if any, were confusing?
2. Which Core fields, if any, felt unnecessary?
3. What information, if any, was missing from the task materials?
4. Any other comments about burden, comprehension, or fatigue?

## Analysis Notes

- Do not combine all items into one total score by default.
- Clarity items, burden items, perceived influence items, and wording-overlap items should be interpreted separately.
- Do not assume reverse-coded items form a validated scale.
- Self-report does not prove influence, copying, confusion, or comprehension.

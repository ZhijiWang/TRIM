# TRIM-HAA Participant-Language Review Report v0.1

## 1. Scope

This report records an author-assisted internal review of participant-facing TRIM-HAA pilot materials. It checks clarity, consistency, and package readiness for external plain-language review.

It does not claim independent participant review. It does not claim ethics approval. It does not make the package ready for recruitment.

- Participant information sheet
- Consent form
- Debrief sheet
- Risk assessment
- Participant workflow
- Participant Core guide
- Participant label guide
- Participant mechanism guide
- AI-review instructions
- Control instructions
- Background questionnaire

- Burden and comprehension questionnaire
- Practice-case protocol
- Interface specification
- Researcher session script

## 3. Main Language Problems Found

- Some documents used internal project terms where participant-facing wording was clearer.
- Response panels needed one consistent naming scheme.
- The label and mechanism guides needed explicit vocabulary-status labels.
- Guide entries needed clearer inclusion rules, near-misses, examples, and boundary notes.
- AI-review language needed to keep AI framed as one possible annotation, not an answer key.

## 4. Revisions Made

- Standardised the panel names: Your earlier response, AI-generated response, and Your current response.
- Kept unchanged answers explicitly acceptable in both second-pass conditions.
- Reaffirmed that agreement with AI is not rewarded.
- Reaffirmed that the study evaluates the workflow, not intelligence or literary ability.
- Added explicit status markers to the label and mechanism guides.
- Expanded label and mechanism entries with inclusion rules, near-misses, short examples, and boundary notes.
- Added this review report and updated the review checklist.

## 5. Terminology Standardisation

Use these participant-facing terms:

- Function label: the category for the role or function identified in the case.
- Rationale mechanism: how the selected text supports the label.
- Uncertainty: how unsure the participant is about the submitted response.
- Alternative pathway: another complete interpretation that may still be possible.
- Your earlier response: the participant's locked first response.
- AI-generated response: the frozen AI response shown only in AI-review cases.
- Your current response: the editable second-pass response.

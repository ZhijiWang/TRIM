# TRIM-HAA Participant Function-Label Guide

Status: `synthetic_demonstration_only`.

The final pilot vocabulary is not frozen in this package. The final study label set is not frozen. Use this guide only to show how a participant-facing label guide should work.

Ethics-submission blocker: final ethics submission requires the actual participant-facing function-label guide for the selected cases. This demonstration guide is not study material for data collection.

Expected session length: approximately 60-90 minutes, including instructions, practice, breaks, annotation tasks, and feedback questions. This duration range is provisional and must be finalised before ethics submission and recruitment.

## How To Use This Guide

A function label is the category that best describes the role or function you identify in the case. Unless the final study guide says otherwise, choose one label only.

If two labels seem plausible, choose the one best supported by your selected primary evidence. Use uncertainty or an alternative pathway when another full interpretation remains possible.

Whether labels are mutually exclusive: final study decision required.

Whether one label only is permitted: final study decision required. Pilot default is one label only.

Whether a no-fit option is available: final study decision required. If available, use it when none of the labels adequately fits.

## Synthetic Demonstration Label Set

The labels below are examples for explaining the task structure only.

### Label: Caution

Plain-language definition: The text shows a character or speaker avoiding risk or checking before acting.

Inclusion rule: Use this label when the selected text shows risk awareness, checking, or careful action before a possible problem.

Exclusion or near-miss: "Mira opened the door quickly because she was excited." This may show eagerness rather than caution.

One short example: "Mira checked the hallway twice before opening the door."

Boundary note: Caution requires evidence of risk awareness or careful checking, not simply slow movement.

### Label: Suspicion

Plain-language definition: The text shows a character or speaker doubting someone or expecting hidden motives.

Inclusion rule: Use this label when the selected text shows distrust, doubt, or concern that someone is hiding something.

Exclusion or near-miss: "Mira forgot where she put the letter." This may show forgetfulness rather than suspicion.

One short example: "Mira hid the letter because she thought Jonah had read the last one."

Boundary note: Suspicion requires evidence of distrust or suspected concealment.

### Label: Reassurance

Plain-language definition: The text shows someone trying to reduce fear, doubt, or tension.

Inclusion rule: Use this label when the selected text shows someone trying to make another person feel safer or less worried.

Exclusion or near-miss: "Jonah changed the topic." This may avoid tension without clearly reducing it.

One short example: "Jonah said the windows were locked and stayed until Mira smiled."

Boundary note: Reassurance needs evidence that the action or speech is meant to make someone feel more secure.

### Label: No Fit

Plain-language definition: None of the available labels adequately describes the case.

Inclusion rule: Use this label only when none of the available labels fits the selected text.

Exclusion or near-miss: A passage with weak evidence of caution should usually be marked uncertain rather than no fit.

One short example: A passage only describes the weather and has no evidence of caution, suspicion, or reassurance.

Boundary note: Use no fit only when the available labels do not work, not simply because the case is difficult.

# TRIM-HAA Participant-Language Review Checklist

Use this checklist before final ethics submission and before recruitment.

## Checklist

- No unexplained technical terms.
- No chain-of-thought language.
- No suggestion that AI is authoritative.
- No suggestion that agreement is rewarded.
- No suggestion that uncertainty is failure.
- No implication that the participant is being tested.
- Function label and rationale mechanism are distinguishable.
- Alternative pathway is understandable.
- Withdrawal language is clear.
- Expected duration is present.
- Optional consent is visibly optional.
- Reading level is appropriate for the participant group.
- All participant-facing documents use consistent terminology.
- The AI-generated response is described as one possible annotation, not an answer key.
- The control condition clearly includes the participant's locked earlier response and no AI-generated material.

# Candidate Construct Note

## Candidate Certainty-Alternative Mismatch

A candidate certainty-alternative mismatch is a descriptive candidate situation in which:

1. a record reports low uncertainty or otherwise presents a strongly closed interpretation;
2. the record selects evidence supporting its primary interpretation;
3. the same packet still preserves at least one explicit alternative interpretation that the record does not adequately address, distinguish, or exclude;
4. the mismatch is presented as a question for independent evaluation, not as proof of error.

This is not equivalent to model wrongness. It is not established by author disagreement alone. Evidence may support more than one interpretation. Low uncertainty is not automatically inappropriate.

The walkthrough only tests whether TRIM-HAA makes the candidate visible.

Preferred language:

The walkthrough demonstrates how TRIM-HAA can represent a candidate certainty-alternative mismatch for subsequent independent evaluation.

Avoid:

- fluent-but-wrong
- hallucinated interpretation
- unlicensed inference as a proven finding
- model failure
- AI overconfidence as an established effect

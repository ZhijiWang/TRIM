# Candidate Construct Note

## Candidate Certainty-Closure Tension

A candidate certainty-closure tension is an author-defined review condition in which:

1. a record reports low uncertainty;
2. the record reports no alternative pathway;
3. the record omits or does not address a packet-anchored element that the walkthrough has designated for review as potentially relevant to interpretive closure;
4. the condition is presented as a review question, not as proof of error.

In this walkthrough, `IAG-SAM-012` is the designated packet-anchored element because it reports later unidentified removal of the small sword. That later intervention may be relevant to narrative closure, sequence, testimony mediation, or post-stabbing action. It does not automatically refute self-stabbing or prove that someone else caused the death.

This is not an adjudicated property of the model record. It does not prove that an alternative interpretation is required. It does not prove that low uncertainty is inappropriate. It does not prove model error. It does not prove overconfidence.

The walkthrough only tests whether TRIM-HAA can preserve and display the submitted fields needed to generate this review question.

## Display Status

The stored display status `candidate_visible` may remain for backward compatibility. It means the configured author-defined review rule was triggered. It does not mean that an independently validated mismatch was detected.

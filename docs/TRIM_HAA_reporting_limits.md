# TRIM-HAA reporting limits

Prototype reports are descriptive. They must not claim:

- AI caused every post-exposure change;
- lower uncertainty means better judgment;
- label convergence means validity;
- copied wording proves internal belief adoption;
- model-generated rationale is faithful chain-of-thought;
- human review is independent when AI was shown first;
- high human-AI agreement proves annotation quality.

Preferred wording:

- "exposure-associated convergence";
- "AI evidence incorporation";
- "increased evidence convergence";
- "submitted rationale";
- "model-generated justificatory record";
- "record-level adoption";
- "descriptive comparison";
- "requires human/ethics-approved pilot data."

Avoid:

- "AI captured the human pathway";
- "synthetic consensus" unless carefully defined;
- "bias" unless an independent quality review supports it.

Reports must keep incorporation, increased convergence, and displacement separate. Incorporation is not automatically beneficial or harmful.
